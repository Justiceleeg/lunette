## DOK 4 - Spiky Points of View (SPOVs)

### SPOV 1

**The "Gatekeeper" Consensus is dead. Musical complexity no longer requires physical dexterity or theoretical fluency. In a live coding environment, sophistication is an *emergent property* of the system, accessible to novices immediately.**

**Elaboration:**
Traditionally, "complex" music was a proxy for "skill" because it required years of physical training to execute. This created a "Gatekeeper" effect: you couldn't make "good" complex music until you paid your dues. Lunette proves this false by leveraging **Emergent Complexity**. A beginner can type a simple Strudel function and generate a complex, professional-sounding rhythm instantly. The difficulty shifts from **Execution** (how do I move my fingers?) to **Curation** (does this sound good?). Therefore, the role of the platform is not to "train" the user to build complexity, but to help them *manage* and *understand* the complexity they can already generate.

## DOK 3 - Insights

-   **Insight 1: Retrospective Theory (The "Reverse-Engineering" Model).**
    Traditional music education operates on a **prescriptive** model: students must learn the rules (scales, rhythm) *before* they can meaningfully compose. Lunette enables a **descriptive** (or retrospective) model: the user creates or forks complex sounds first via intuition or hacking, and the AI Teacher's role is to "discover" and label the theory *already present* in that code. In this model, music theory is not a prerequisite for creation; it is a vocabulary for understanding what you have already created.
